My unwarranted notes on philosophy

Philosophy is the generalised study of any thing. Things are of two kinds: Real things and abstract things. Real things are also termed as entities. Entities are things that exists. To exist is to be the part of reality. Reality is the totatlity of entities and their descriptions.

The study of reality (to find what things are entities and what is their description) is called metaphysics. Metaphysics is often regarded as the first philosophy.

Agents are entities that are able to experience reality and do reasoning. You are an agent. Objects are entities other than agents. A chair is an object.

Abstract things are not existent in reality as entities but still they can be conceived and described by agents. For example, spiderman, electric fields, point particles, line, etc.

Properties are the descriptions of things. Experience and reasoning are the processes through which an agent forms beliefs. A belief is the attitude (judgement) held by an agent about any description of properties of a thing. A belief expressed in some language is called a proposition.

A proposition is qualifiied as true and be called a truth if it corresponds to reality (the correspondence theory of truth) or is reasoned based on other truths or definitions (the coherence theory of truth). Reasoning is the act of forming proposition from other propositions. The initial propositions from where reasoning starts are called premises and the inferred propositions are called conclusions. The premises proposed to derive some conclusions from it is called an argument.

Types of arguments:
Strong = Certainly or probably true conclusion
Valid = Certainly true conclusion
Sound = Valid and all premises are true.
Cogent = Strong and all premises are true.
